# Tamazia · Privacy Policy
**Version v2026-05 · Last updated: 2026-05-19**

This Privacy Policy explains how Tamazia Ltd ("Tamazia", "we", "us") collects, uses and protects personal data. It covers visitors to tamazia.co.uk, recipients of Tamazia commercial communications, and clients of Tamazia's services. It satisfies the disclosure requirements of UK GDPR Article 13 and Article 14, and the equivalent provisions of the EU General Data Protection Regulation where it applies.

## 2. What personal data we process and how we got it (Article 13 and Article 14)

We process the following categories of personal data.

**Identification and contact data**: name, work email, work phone, job title, employer name and address, professional profile URLs (LinkedIn, company website). For website visitors who submit a form: the contact details supplied. For prospects: identification and contact details obtained from public corporate sources (company websites, Companies House, regulator registers, public news), business directories operating under their own lawful basis, and the recipient's own public posts on professional networks.

**Engagement data**: emails sent to and received from prospects, replies, meeting notes, audit results addressed to a contact's domain, and ordinary business communication.

**Website usage data**: IP address, browser type, pages visited, referring URL, time on page, device type. Cookies that are not strictly necessary are set only with explicit prior consent.

**Service data**: details of the services we are providing or being asked to provide, content reviewed during regulatory signal scans, and the results of those scans (which may incidentally identify the contact details of named persons featured on the client's site).

We never knowingly collect personal data relating to children under 13.

## 3. Legal basis for processing

We rely on one or more of the following legal bases under UK GDPR Article 6 (and EU GDPR Article 6 where it applies).

**Legitimate Interest** (Article 6(1)(f)): for B2B outreach to professional contacts at corporate addresses identifiable in their public role, where the proposed service is materially relevant to the recipient's professional responsibilities and the impact on the recipient's privacy is limited. We document the legitimate interest balancing test in our internal records. Recipients always have an unconditional right to object (clause 5).

**Performance of contract** (Article 6(1)(b)): for processing necessary to deliver services to a client, including invoicing and engagement administration.

**Legal obligation** (Article 6(1)(c)): for compliance with tax, accounting and statutory record-keeping obligations.

**Consent** (Article 6(1)(a)): for non-essential cookies, marketing analytics, and where we expressly request consent in any other context. Consent can be withdrawn at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.

We do not process special category personal data (race, health, religion, biometric or genetic data) in the ordinary course of business. Where this is unavoidable (for example regulator references in a healthcare sector scan), we apply Article 9 conditions where applicable and minimise processing.

## 4. How we use personal data

We use personal data to: provide and improve our services; communicate with clients, prospects and partners; respond to enquiries; produce Regulatory Signal Scans for clients; maintain financial and statutory records; protect the security of our systems; understand and improve our website. We do not sell personal data and we do not engage in automated decision-making with legal or similarly significant effect on individuals.

## 5. Your rights (Articles 15 to 22)

You have the following rights, which you may exercise free of charge by writing to aman@tamazia.co.uk.

**Article 15 right of access**: confirmation of whether we process your data and, if so, a copy with the additional information required by Article 15.

**Article 16 right to rectification**: correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.

**Article 17 right to erasure** (right to be forgotten): deletion of your data where one of the Article 17 grounds applies. Where you ask us to stop marketing to you, we add your email to a permanent suppression list to prevent re-contact; the suppression record itself is retained as evidence of compliance.

**Article 18 right to restriction**: temporary restriction of processing during dispute.

**Article 20 right to data portability**: a structured, commonly used, machine-readable copy of data you provided to us where processing is based on consent or contract and is carried out by automated means.

**Article 21 right to object**: an unconditional right to object to direct marketing. A more limited right to object on legitimate-interest grounds in other contexts, balanced against our compelling legitimate interest where applicable.

**Article 22 rights related to automated decision-making**: we do not carry out automated decision-making with legal or similarly significant effect on individuals.

We respond within one calendar month of receipt of a verified request. If a request is manifestly unfounded or excessive we may charge a reasonable fee or decline.

You also have the **right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office** at ico.org.uk/make-a-complaint or by post to Information Commissioner's Office, Wycliffe House, Water Lane, Wilmslow SK9 5AF.

## 6. Legitimate Interest assessment for B2B outreach

We outreach to professional contacts at corporate addresses. Before each campaign we document a Legitimate Interest assessment covering: the corporate addresses involved, the relevance of the proposed service to the recipient's documented professional responsibilities, the reasonable expectation of the recipient that they may be contacted by a regulated-services adviser in their sector, the safeguards we apply, and the unconditional opt-out we provide. We do not contact private individuals at personal addresses on this basis. We do not target consumers.

---

## 7. Data Retention

We retain personal data for the periods needed to fulfil the purposes described above. Concretely: prospect data is retained while there is a reasonable prospect of a business relationship and for up to six (6) months after last meaningful interaction; client engagement records are retained for six (6) years after the end of the engagement to support legitimate accounting and tax obligations under UK law; suppression list entries are retained indefinitely to enforce the opt-out; website usage logs (in aggregate) for thirteen (13) months; financial records for six (6) full UK tax years; payroll and personnel data per HMRC and statutory requirements; legal correspondence and regulatory submissions for the longer of seven (7) years and the relevant limitation period.

## 11. Cookies

The Tamazia website sets the following cookie categories.

**Strictly necessary**: session state, security tokens and consent record. Set without consent.

**Functional**: language preference, dark mode preference. Set with consent.

**Analytics**: aggregated usage statistics via a privacy-preserving analytics provider (Plausible.io or equivalent), set with consent.

**Marketing**: none currently set.

The cookie banner allows accept, reject and granular preference. Reject is presented with equal prominence to accept. Consent can be withdrawn from the footer link "Cookie preferences" at any time. We retain consent records for thirteen (13) months.
